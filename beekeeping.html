Introduction to beekeeping

This one-day course is designed as an introduction for potential new beekeepers with less than 2 years of experience.

The course will run from 10am till 4pm:

The morning is a classroom lecture, the afternoon is a practical demonstration in the hives to allow participants "hands on" experience. We will cover all aspects of hive inspection so that you understand and learn to "read" your bees.

Protective clothing will be supplied if required although bring boots if possible.

The price of the course is £30.00/person (please subscribe to our newsletter for discounts and monthly offers).

Weather and wildlife in Scotland

Scotland's climate is moderate and very changeable, and contrary to popular belief, it can get very hot and cold too. Average rainfall in certain parts of the country can exceed 3000 mm/year but as the Scottish saying states: "There is no such thing as bad weather, only bad clothing!"

Scotland is home to plenty of species which makes this small country rich and diverse in terms of wildlife. Agriculture, forestry, increase of infrastructure and urban development altogether had a strong impact on these numbers in the second half of the 20th century. Fortunately, we realised that we could not go on with these practices without consequences, and as a result of synchronized efforts in the past years, the numbers are showing signs of improvement. This change in our attitude can help turn back these negative tendencies and create a better World!
